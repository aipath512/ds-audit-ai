What it is: AEO structures your content so AI assistants — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Siri — extract it as a direct answer without the user clicking a link.

Why it matters: 60%+ of AI responses cite zero external links. If your content isn't AEO-optimised, you're invisible in the answer layer — even if you rank #1 on Google.

Examples of AEO signals: FAQ Schema · HowTo Schema · Answer-First paragraphs · Featured Snippet formatting · Question-intent URLs · Speakable Schema · Direct Definition Blocks

Real impact: A site with FAQ schema + answer-first structure is 4× more likely to be quoted in ChatGPT Browse responses than a site without.

What it is: GEO makes your brand entity discoverable, citable, and trustworthy to the generative AI layer — the infrastructure that LLMs use to decide WHO to mention.

Why it matters: Google, Claude, Gemini and ChatGPT all resolve brand identity through entity graphs. Without a declared entity, you are a string of text — not a brand. You cannot be cited with confidence.

Examples of GEO signals: llms.txt · ai.json · entities.json · JSON-LD entity graph · Organization Schema · sameAs links to Wikipedia/Wikidata · Press mentions · LinkedIn entity verification · proof.json

Real impact: 5thElement.ai scores 96/100 on llms.txt — one of fewer than 2,000 domains globally with a fully declared AI entity stack.

What it is: AIO covers the full stack of signals that determine how AI systems understand, rank, and reproduce your content — from crawler permissions to topical authority to RAG-readiness.

Why it matters: LLMs don't just read your page — they decide whether your content deserves to be in their training corpus and retrieval layer. AIO is the difference between being a source and being invisible.

Examples of AIO signals: Topical Authority depth · Semantic keyword clusters · E-E-A-T score · RAG-ready content format · Corpus inclusion signals · ChatGPT Browse visibility · Gemini grounding eligibility · Claude source indexing

Real impact: Sites with RAG-ready structure (chunked, cited, dense paragraphs) are 6× more likely to appear in Perplexity cited sources.

What it is: SEO remains the technical and content foundation. In the AI era, SEO signals are the substrate that AI crawlers use to assess domain authority, content freshness, and technical trustworthiness.

Why it matters: A site with broken Core Web Vitals, missing meta tags, and toxic backlinks will score poorly on AI discovery too — because AI systems use SEO signals as trust proxies.

Examples of SEO signals: Title tags · Meta descriptions · H1–H6 hierarchy · Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, INP) · Mobile responsiveness · HTTPS · XML sitemap · Internal link architecture · Backlink authority · Duplicate content · Canonical tags

Real impact: Google's AI Overviews draw 73% of their cited sources from pages that also rank in the top 10 organic results.

What it is: On-Page signals are everything visible and coded within a single page — title tags, schema markup, heading structure, content quality, and answer formatting.

Covers signals from: AEO (FAQ/HowTo schema, answer-first structure) · SEO (title, meta, H1-H6, word count) · AIO (topical depth, RAG format, E-E-A-T) · GEO (OG tags, entity density, named entities)

What it is: Off-Page signals come from outside your domain — backlinks, press mentions, AI citations, social proof, and third-party entity verification.

Covers signals from: SEO (backlinks, DA, toxic ratio) · AEO (Knowledge Panel, PAA coverage) · GEO (Wikipedia entity, Crunchbase, LinkedIn verification, press AI-indexed) · AIO (Perplexity citations, ChatGPT visibility, Gemini grounding)

What it is: On-Site signals span your entire domain infrastructure — file architecture, crawl configuration, AI governance files, and technical health — not limited to a single page.

Covers signals from: GEO (llms.txt, ai.json, entities.json, robots.txt) · SEO (sitemap, canonicals, HTTPS, crawl budget) · AIO (AI crawl permissions, allow-lane-matrix) · AI Signals (proof.json, session.json, governance.json)

What it is: Off-Site AI signals are your presence in external AI ecosystems — LLM knowledge graphs, vector databases, blockchain IP registries, and AI answer layers that exist independently of your website.

Covers signals from: AI Signals (IPFS/Blockchain registration, Gemini grounding verified, AI Knowledge Graph entry, LLM embedding proximity, Cross-AI consistency) · GEO (external entity links quality, Wikipedia/Wikidata anchor)
